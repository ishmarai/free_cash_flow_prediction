# Methodology: Data Preprocessing, Variable Formulation, and Feature Engineering

This section details the empirical research design, data cleaning pipeline, formulation of the target variable (Free Cash Flow), and the feature engineering process used to build the panel dataset for forecasting.

---

## 1. Data Source and Sample Selection

The primary data source for the core financial panel (`hydro.csv`) is **System X**, an institutional-grade financial analytics terminal developed by SMTM Capital (https://smtmcapital.com.np/products/systemX/). System X is a premier data infrastructure widely utilized by mutual funds, investment banks, and brokers in Nepal, ensuring the highest level of data fidelity and institutional rigor for this study. The extracted raw database covers Nepalese hydropower companies listed on the Nepal Stock Exchange (NEPSE) spanning the fiscal years 2010/11 to 2025/26. The following systematic filters were applied to the raw quarterly financial statements to construct the final annual panel dataset:

1.  **Temporal Aggregation (Annual Level)**:
    Only Quarter 4 (year-end) reports were selected to align the analysis with annual financial reporting cycles and avoid frequency mismatches with annual forecasting targets.
2.  **Report Type De-duplication**:
    For firm-years where both preliminary (Published) and audited (Audited) year-end statements were available, **Audited** reports were prioritized to maximize data accuracy and disclosure completeness.
3.  **Target and Lag Availability Filter**:
    Observations were removed if they lacked the consecutive historical data required to calculate capital expenditures ($CapEx$), changes in working capital ($\Delta WC$), or lag features. 

The final clean panel dataset consists of **477 firm-year observations across 90 unique NEPSE-listed hydropower companies**.

### 1.1 Reproducible Data Collection: NEA Annual Reports
To ensure transparency for reviewers, the collection of sector-level and macroeconomic data is documented. The official Nepal Electricity Authority (NEA) Annual Reports spanning from 2009/10 to 2024/25 were manually downloaded from the official NEA website (https://nea.org.np). Manual collection was necessary due to strict Web Application Firewall (WAF) and bot-protection mechanisms deployed on the NEA server, which blocked automated scraping attempts. The downloaded PDF documents are stored locally for subsequent manual data extraction (e.g., for system-wide hydrology indicators and bulk power purchase tariffs).

## 2. Target Variable Formulation: Free Cash Flow (FCF)

Free Cash Flow ($FCF$) represents the net cash generated by the firm that is available for distribution to all capital providers (debt and equity). For each firm $i$ in fiscal year $t$, FCF is formulated as:

$$FCF_{i,t} = EBIT_{i,t} \times (1 - T_{i,t}) + D\&A_{i,t} - CapEx_{i,t} - \Delta WC_{i,t}$$

Where the components are extracted and calculated from the financial statements as follows:

*   **Operating Profit ($EBIT_{i,t}$)**: Sourced directly from the operating profit line item of the income statement.
*   **Effective Tax Rate ($T_{i,t}$)**: Calculated dynamically to reflect tax holidays and regime differences:
    $$T_{i,t} = \begin{cases} \max\left(0, \min\left(1, \frac{PBT_{i,t} - PAT_{i,t}}{PBT_{i,t}}\right)\right) & \text{if } PBT_{i,t} > 0 \\ 0 & \text{if } PBT_{i,t} \le 0 \end{cases}$$
    where $PBT$ is Profit Before Tax and $PAT$ is Profit After Tax.
*   **Depreciation & Amortization ($D\&A_{i,t}$)**: The period depreciation expense sourced from the income statement notes.
*   **Capital Expenditures ($CapEx_{i,t}$)**: Calculated from the change in Gross Fixed Assets ($FixedAssets$) and Capital Work-in-Progress ($WIP$):
    $$CapEx_{i,t} = (FixedAssets_{i,t} + WIP_{i,t}) - (FixedAssets_{i,t-1} + WIP_{i,t-1})$$
    *Note: By combining Gross Fixed Assets and WIP, we accurately capture capital disbursements during the pre-COD construction phase.*
*   **Change in Working Capital ($\Delta WC_{i,t}$)**: Calculated as:
    $$\Delta WC_{i,t} = WorkingCapital_{i,t} - WorkingCapital_{i,t-1}$$
    where $WorkingCapital_{i,t} = CurrentAssets_{i,t} - CurrentLiabilities_{i,t}$.

## 3. Feature Engineering and Predictors

To capture the distinct financial, operational, and structural dynamics of hydropower projects, a panel of **30 features** was engineered to train the machine learning models. These are categorized into structural, financial, and time-series/lag features:

### 3.1. Structural & Lifecycle Features
*   **COD Year ($COD\_Year_i$)**: The first fiscal year in the dataset where firm $i$ records positive electricity sales ($EnergySales_i > 0$).
*   **Years Since COD ($Years\_Since\_COD_{i,t}$)**: 
    $$Years\_Since\_COD_{i,t} = Year\_Int_{i,t} - COD\_Year_i$$
    This continuous variable captures the life stage of the project (construction phase is negative, operational phase is positive).
*   **Pre/Post-COD Status ($Pre\_Post\_COD_{i,t}$)**: A binary indicator set to `0` if the firm is in the construction phase ($Year\_Int < COD\_Year$) and `1` if it is operational ($Year\_Int \ge COD\_Year$).

### 3.2. Core Financial Ratios
*   **EBIT Margin**: $\frac{EBIT}{Revenue}$ (measures operating efficiency)
*   **Net Profit Margin (NPM)**: $\frac{PAT}{Revenue}$ (measures overall profitability)
*   **Debt Ratio**: $\frac{TotalDebt}{TotalAssets}$ (leverage measure, where $TotalDebt = LtLiabilities + CurrentLiabilities$)
*   **Current Ratio**: $\frac{CurrentAssets}{CurrentLiabilities}$ (liquidity measure)
*   **Return on Assets (ROA)**: $\frac{PAT}{TotalAssets}$
*   **Return on Equity (ROE)**: $\frac{PAT}{TotalEquity}$ (where $TotalEquity = PaidUpCapital + Premium + Reserves$)
*   **Asset Turnover**: $\frac{Revenue}{TotalAssets}$ (efficiency of asset utilization)
*   **Interest Burden**: $\frac{Interest}{EBIT}$ (debt service pressure)

### 3.3. Lag and Time-Series Dynamics
*   **Revenue Growth**: $\frac{Revenue_t - Revenue_{t-1}}{Revenue_{t-1}}$
*   **EBIT Growth**: $\frac{EBIT_t - EBIT_{t-1}}{EBIT_{t-1}}$
*   **Lags**: First lags of top-line revenue and target variable: $Revenue_{t-1}$ (`Revenue_Lag1`), $FCF_{t-1}$ (`FCF_Lag1`), and $RevenueGrowth_{t-1}$ (`Revenue_Growth_Lag1`).
*   **Rolling Averages**: 3-year rolling averages of revenue growth (`Rolling3_Avg_Rev_Growth`) and EBIT margin (`Rolling3_Avg_Ebit_Margin`) to capture multi-year trends and smooth out hydrological seasonality.
